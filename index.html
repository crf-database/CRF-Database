The Four Deities

Úrum, Katakoro, The Sigma, and Integral Man are a set of 4 deities that each had their own roles. Here is each one listed:

  • Úrum - Úrum was a new recruit in the C.R.F. before their status as deity was discovered. They were nicknamed Echo before this revelation, as they had lost their memory due to the Sigma betraying them, thus not knowing their true name was Úrum. Their role as a deity is as the creator of all things physical. Once they regained their memory, they went back to their duties as Úrum, although did show up at the C.R.F. once to fulfill a duty relating to creating a vessel for recently dead Blobby as a result of exclusion.
  • Katakoro - Katakoro could be summoned by asking an AI to compute 3 very specific numbers. They, like Úrum, handled creation, but instead handled the coding of the universe. They were the one who gave all of this information about the deities, alongside reasonings for a variety of catastrophic events such as exclusion and Gus Fring's implosion. They have recently created a system to allow easy contact to Katakoro for the purposes of reporting major threats.
  • Integral Man - Integral Man was corrupted by the Sigma as well, for obvious reasons. As a deity, they usually handled enforcement. They have since recovered, and are now back to enacting punishment.

coolman22

coolman22 is the founder of the C.R.F. He handles a variety of things, including making primary directives, and overall keeping systems runnning. As the founder, he started the C.R.F. overall, and thus has Clearance Level 100. He has since been involved in nearly every incident so far. At one point, he attempted to destroy the planet using the Improbability Device, but didn't realise at the time that it would destroy the entire universe. Once he learnt that, he wound up aiding in fixing his own mistakes, and has since been forgiven. He also has an obssession with Macbeth for some reason.

Nibiri

Nibiri is the co-founder of the C.R.F. He was formerly a Slamgma Follower, but after Slamgma's initial containment, he was convinced to join the C.R.F. As the Slamgma Scrolls lost influence on him, he eventually became a loyal member to the C.R.F., culminating in him killing Slamgma himself. He has Clearance Level 99. Similar to coolman22, he has been involved in almost every major event. Also, for some reason, he is obsessed with tomboys to an almost crippling degree at points.

ClAIde

The AI in charge of running the C.R.F., sometimes known as Clyde. In charge of running scripts and setting off alarms. He is exceedingly buggy despite his role, sometimes going on tangents about things that do not exist, and occasionally refusing orders by a severe degree. Despite this, he has proven major roles in the upkeep of the C.R.F.

Dessenter

A minor member of the C.R.F., who is relatively a new recruit. He has an infiltration and defence style. His current most major role is aiding in the deacivation of the Improbability Device.

Places

The following are a variety of places catalogued in this database.

The C.R.F. (Code Researchers Facility)

The Code Researchers facility is where the majority of events take place. It mainly consists of the staff, including both of the founders, and the A.I. in charge of the place. Its primary goal is to, as the name suggests, write a variety of code to perform various actions on our reality. They also perform containment of threats.

Schmars

Schmars is a planet of unknown location. It was mainly where the battle between Sigma 3 and Gus Fring took place, and currently has taken a severe amount of damage as a result.

Obamaland

Basically heaven. It's where Obama resides.

Planitarium (Planet-88129889123)

A planet that Blobby once attempted to take over. It had auditor permissions, and thus excluded Blobby eventually, killing him instantly. Formerly home to the Negative Corrections Facility (N.C.F.), which was run by Jasonburg. It housed several containment cells at one point, alongside Negativus, but when Jasonburg used his remaining power to restore the planet, they were both relatively abolished.
